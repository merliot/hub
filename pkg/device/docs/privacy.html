PRIVACY

Privacy is a main feature of Merliot Hub.

Consider consumer-grade Smart devices such as Smart security cameras, Smart lights, and Smart thermostats. These are not private. They have one thing in common: they dial home to the device manufacturer's central hub. For each manufacturer, you'll install a phone app which connects to the same central hub. Your data is unencrypted on the manufacturer's side of the hub. Your data is not private. The device manufacturer can analyze, track, store, share, and sell your data. Your data plus your profile associated with the device purchase and app signup make _you_ the product.

Merliot Hub is a private alternative. It’s private by switching from a central-hub architecture to a distributed-hub architecture. There is no central Merliot Hub. Each user of Merliot Hub installs their own hub. A hub can be installed on a local resource such as a laptop or Raspberry Pi. Alternatively, it can be installed on the cloud (for free in some cases). Each user’s hub is independent from others’. There is no central hub. No central point to compromise, tap, exploit, or profit from.

The trade off for privacy is convenience. You must install and maintain your own Merliot Hub and you must to build your own devices.
